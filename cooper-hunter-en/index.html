The Diagnosis

Cooper & Hunter has product and brand, but it's blind to the market and weak where the decision happens today.

The HVAC market in the United States is large, fragmented and data-driven: who's building, who's renovating, who complains, who influences, and what artificial intelligence answers when someone asks about air conditioning. Today Cooper & Hunter has neither eyes nor hands on these fronts.

01

Invisible opportunity

Building permits reveal who will need HVAC before the competitor finds out. Without capturing this data by location, the company only reacts, it never anticipates.

02

Unmonitored competition

What HVAC companies do on Google Business and Instagram (reviews, posts, offers) is open information, but nobody is reading it systematically.

03

Influence off the radar

There are influencers and content creators moving the HVAC market. Cooper & Hunter has no map of who they are, what they say, and who is worth a partnership.

04

Reputation in the dark

Complaints and mentions on forums and online communities shape the opinion of whoever is researching. Without monitoring, the brand finds out about the problem too late, once it has already become a narrative.

05

Poorly positioned in AI

When someone asks ChatGPT, Gemini or Perplexity about HVAC, Cooper & Hunter shows up poorly or doesn't show up at all. This is the new recommendation counter, and the brand is out of it.

06

Everything manual and disconnected

Even what's done today depends on someone stopping to do it. Nothing runs on its own, nothing connects, and the data collected doesn't turn into a decision or an automatic action.

The Proposal

An AI structure with eyes on the market, a shield on reputation and presence where the decision happens.

Everything built inside Cooper & Hunter, with Cooper & Hunter's brand, on Cooper & Hunter's infrastructure. The structure runs outside the AI model, which makes it portable: if tomorrow it makes sense to switch the intelligence engine, you switch without rebuilding anything. At the end, the company runs the ecosystem without depending on the outside.

01

Agent ecosystem and AI CEO

The core of the project: the complete Claude Code agent structure, with an AI CEO orchestrating departments. It's the brain that coordinates all the fronts below.

  • AI CEO plus agents by area
  • Cooper & Hunter knowledge base interconnected
  • Security agent scanning for exposed keys and APIs
  • Operated directly in Claude Code, in natural language
02

Intelligence portal on Vercel

A dedicated portal where all collected data becomes dashboards, lists and alerts. It's the single screen where Cooper & Hunter sees market, competition and reputation.

  • Portal hosted on Vercel (Cooper & Hunter account)
  • Repository on GitHub (Cooper & Hunter account)
  • Database on Supabase (Cooper & Hunter account)
  • Dashboards for opportunity, competition and reputation
03

Permit intelligence by location

Capture of building permits in the chosen areas, revealing construction and renovation that will demand HVAC. It starts with the priority locations and grows on demand.

  • Capture by location, respecting each one's rules
  • Initial areas defined at kickoff
  • New locations added as needed
  • Opportunities landing straight in the portal
04

Google Business and Instagram radar

Systematic reading of HVAC companies on Google Business and Instagram, plus the map of influencers and content creators in the niche.

  • Monitoring of HVAC companies on Google Business
  • Collection of company profiles on Instagram
  • Map of HVAC market influencers
  • Map of who creates articles and content in the sector
05

Visibility in search and AI (GEO)

Make Cooper & Hunter found and recommended when someone asks ChatGPT, Gemini or Perplexity about HVAC, and improve presence in search.

  • Diagnosis of how the brand shows up in the AIs today
  • Actions to get cited and recommended in the answers
  • Strengthening of presence and authority
  • Tracking of the evolution over time
06

Everything running on its own, in routines on Claude Desktop

What ties the whole project together: the fronts above don't depend on someone pressing a button. They become routines that run on their own on Claude Desktop, collect data, update the portal and alert the team. And any adjustment or new location is requested in natural language, straight in Claude Code, and the agents incorporate it.

  • Automatic collection and update routines
  • Opportunity and reputation alerts for the team
  • New areas and adjustments requested in natural language in Claude Code
  • A structure that grows without needing a rebuild

Important: the scraper grows by location, not all at once

You can't sweep the entire United States at once, because each location has different rules from the next for access to permits and public data. The right path, and what's in this proposal, is to choose the priority locations at the start and add others as the business needs them. Each new area is incorporated by the agents from a simple request in Claude Code. The structure is born ready to grow.

Complementary stage · Reputation on forums and communities (ready in 30 days)

The reputation work comes as a complementary stage, ready in 30 days: monitoring of brand mentions and complaints on forums and online communities, with real-time alerts, authentic and managed presence (real responses and useful content) and press release support. Reputation built sustainably, with no risk of platform penalties.
